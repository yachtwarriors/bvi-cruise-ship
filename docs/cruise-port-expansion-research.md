# Cruise Port Expansion Research

**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Purpose:** Identify the best English-speaking cruise port destinations for building a cruise ship schedule + crowd tracker tool (like bvicruiseshipschedule.com)

---

## Evaluation Criteria

1. English-speaking (or English is the primary tourist language)
2. Small enough that cruise ship arrivals significantly impact the experience
3. High cruise ship traffic volume
4. Popular with American tourists
5. Keyword search volume + low competition (SEO opportunity)
6. No existing niche local tool doing what we do (crowd predictions, not just schedule)

---

## Tier 1: Best Expansion Targets

### 1. Key West, Florida

**The strongest candidate by far.**

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Annual ship calls | ~400 (down from 417 in 2019; fluctuates due to political fights) |
| Annual passengers | ~900,000 |
| Population | 25,000 |
| Crowd impact | Up to 6,000 cruise visitors/day in a 2x4 mile island |
| Language | English |
| American tourists | Nearly 100% |

**Why crowds matter:** Key West is tiny (2x4 miles). The old town is walkable and gets absolutely slammed when ships are in. Voters passed a referendum in 2020 to limit cruise ships (63% in favor), but DeSantis overturned it with state law. The overcrowding debate is ongoing and intense -- people actively search for "when are cruise ships in Key West" to AVOID them.

**Keyword opportunity:**
| Keyword | US Vol | KD |
|---------|--------|----|
| key west cruise ship schedule | 800 | 2 |
| key west cruise port | 1,400 | 2 |
| key west cruise | 1,800 | 4 |
| cruise ship schedule key west | 300 | 1 |
| key west cruise ship schedule 2025 | 300 | -- |
| **Total cluster** | **~4,000+** | |

**SERP competition:** Dominated by generic aggregators (CruiseMapper DR 70, CruiseTimetables DR 45, CruiseDig DR 33). No niche local tool with crowd predictions. The local site thekeywester.com (DR 25) has a basic schedule page ranking #11 with only 2 refdomains. Facebook groups fill positions 5 and 19 -- clear demand gap.

**Existing schedule tools:**
- cruisemapper.com (#1) -- generic, no crowd analysis
- cruisetimetables.com (#2) -- generic table
- thekeywester.com (#11) -- basic embedded schedule, no crowd data
- caribenautical.com (#6, DR 0) -- port agent site
- No crowd prediction tool exists

**Verdict: DO THIS FIRST.** 800 vol/mo for the schedule keyword alone, 1,400 for cruise port, total cluster 4,000+. KD 2 across the board. No competitor does crowd predictions. The political controversy around cruise overcrowding means people actively want to know when ships are in port. This is essentially BVI Cruise Ship Schedule v2 for a market 5x larger.

### 2. Grand Cayman (George Town)

**Strong candidate -- tender port with massive crowd impact.**

| Metric | Data |

Stingray City Sandbar, the top attraction, becomes dangerously overcrowded on multi-ship days. Seven Mile Beach gets packed. Locals are vocal about overtourism.

**SERP competition:** Port Authority (caymanport.com, DR 33) ranks #1 with 38 refdomains -- but it's a clunky government schedule tool, no crowd analysis. CruiseMapper #2. Facebook groups at positions 5-6. No crowd prediction tool exists.

**Existing schedule tools:**
- caymanport.com (#1) -- official port authority, basic calendar
- cruisemapper.com (#2) -- generic

**Verdict: STRONG #2.** The "cruise port" keyword at 1,600 vol suggests people want port info broadly (not just schedule), which means port guide + crowd tracker content pages would capture significant traffic. ### 3. Bermuda (Kings Wharf / Royal Naval Dockyard)

**Good candidate -- isolated island, seasonal, very American.**

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Annual ship calls | ~120-140 (seasonal: April-November only) |
| Annual passengers | ~500,000-600,000 |
| Population | 64,000 |
| Crowd impact | Dockyard area gets crushed; Horseshoe Bay Beach overwhelmed |
| Language | English (British Overseas Territory) |
| American tourists | Dominant -- most cruises from NYC/NJ/Boston |

**Why crowds matter:** Bermuda is 21 square miles total. The Dockyard cruise port area is tiny. When multiple ships dock (Norwegian Aqua alone = 3,500+ passengers), the Dockyard shops and attractions are standing room only. Horseshoe Bay Beach, reachable by bus, gets mobbed. Ships often stay 2-3 nights (unlike Caribbean 1-day calls), meaning sustained crowd impact.

**Keyword opportunity:**
| Keyword | US Vol | KD |
|---------|--------|----|
| bermuda cruise port | 800 | 0 |
| bermuda cruise ship schedule | 150 | 0 |
| bermuda cruise schedule | 40 | 4 |
| **Total cluster** | **~1,200+** | |

**SERP competition:** CruiseMapper #1 (DR 70, 11 refdomains). Bermuda tourism authority has a PDF schedule ranking #2 (not interactive). Marine & Ports gov site at #3. No dedicated schedule tool. No crowd predictions.

**Existing schedule tools:**
- gotobermuda.com -- static PDF (non-interactive)
- marineandports.bm -- static PDF
- cruisemapper.com -- generic
- No interactive schedule tool exists
- No crowd prediction tool exists

**Verdict: GOOD OPPORTUNITY.** KD 0 for "bermuda cruise port" at 800 vol is remarkable. The entire SERP is PDFs and generic aggregators. But lower overall volume than Key West or Grand Cayman, and the seasonal nature (Apr-Nov) means 5 months/year of zero traffic. Still worth building -- especially as a multi-port platform play.

## Tier 2: Strong Potential

### 4. Juneau, Alaska

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Annual ship calls | ~600+ (May-September season) |
| Annual passengers | 1,700,000 (2025) |
| Population | 32,000 |
| Crowd impact | 21,000 tourists on peak days vs. 32,000 residents; new 5-ship/day cap |

**Why crowds matter:** Juneau imposed a 5-ship daily cap and 16,000 passenger daily limit starting 2026 due to overwhelming community pushback. The ratio of cruise visitors to residents on peak days is brutal (21,000 tourists vs 32,000 locals). Only accessible by air/sea -- no road in.

**Keyword opportunity:**
| Keyword | US Vol | KD |
|---------|--------|----|
| juneau cruise port | 800 | 2 |
| juneau cruise ship schedule | 150 | 3 |
| **Total cluster** | **~1,200+** | |

**SERP competition:** Travel Juneau (traveljuneau.com, DR 57) has a ship calendar ranking #1. Cruise Line Agencies of Alaska (claalaska.com, DR 37) has PDFs. CruiseMapper #3. Competition is slightly tougher because Travel Juneau's calendar is functional (not just a PDF).

**Existing schedule tools:**
- traveljuneau.com -- functional calendar (official tourism board)
- claalaska.com -- PDF schedules
- No crowd prediction tool exists

**Challenge:** Seasonal (May-Sep). Travel Juneau already has a decent calendar tool. But nobody does crowd predictions for Mendenhall Glacier, downtown, or the Juneau tram. The new ship limits make crowd data MORE relevant, not less.

## Revenue / Monetization Notes

- Each port tool can run ads (cruise excursion affiliates, local tour operators)
- Key West has MASSIVE shore excursion ad market (Fury Water Adventures, Dry Tortugas ferry, sunset cruises)
- Grand Cayman has Stingray City tours, snorkeling, etc.
- Cross-linking between port tools builds domain authority
- Consider: one multi-port domain (cruiseportcrowds.com?) vs. individual domains per port
  - Individual domains: better for local SEO, easier to sell/spin off
  - Multi-port domain: one domain to build authority on, easier to maintain
  - **Recommendation:** Start with individual domains (like BVI model), consider consolidation later
